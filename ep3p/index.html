3-Week Ride Home Rule Tracker

Check off each game after you've held the Ride Home Rule. One check = car ride home was conversation-free for performance analysis. Music or silence only.
The Rule: The car ride home is a decompression container — not a debrief. No performance analysis, no "what happened," no "you seemed distracted." You can connect as a parent. That's not the same as reviewing the game.

Conversation Starter Bank

Language patterns for when the window opens — typically 24 hours after a difficult game, or when your athlete initiates.
The Script That Changes Everything
Car ride home. Tough loss. Athlete is quiet. You want to help.
"I liked watching you compete today." Then nothing.
Opens the window
"I noticed [specific behavior]. What were you thinking in that moment?" Leads with observation, not judgment. Invites their interpretation first.
"That stretch in the third quarter looked different from your usual. What was going on for you?" Specific and curious — not evaluative. Names what was seen, asks for their read.
"What felt like it worked today, even if the result didn't go the way you wanted?" Redirects from outcome to process. Reinforces mastery-focus even after a loss.
"Is there anything you want to talk through? No pressure either way." Preserves their autonomy. Makes the conversation optional — which usually makes them more likely to engage.
"I had some thoughts about what I saw. Do you want to hear them, or would it be more useful to hear yours first?" Names that you have observations while offering them the lead. Collaborative, not directive.
"How are you feeling about it today — different from right after?" Signals the 24-hour window intentionally. Opens reflection without forcing it.
Tends to close it
"You know you can't [behavior] at this level." Evaluative + prescriptive. Positions parent as judge. Usually triggers defensiveness, not reflection.
"I just want to understand what happened." Sounds neutral — but signals an agenda. The word "just" rarely precedes a genuinely open question.
"You seemed distracted / off / in your head out there." Labels their internal state from the outside. Athletes frequently experience this as invalidating, not insightful.
"I'm not criticizing — I'm just saying..." The defensive opener signals exactly what it claims to deny. The athlete stops listening at "I'm not criticizing."
"What were you thinking?" (right after the game) Rhetorical when the window isn't open. Asks for cognitive processing during emotional activation — the timing is wrong.
When to escalate: The 24-hour delay is for performance processing. If your athlete is showing signs of genuine distress — not disappointment, but sustained withdrawal, statements about quitting, or anything that concerns you as a parent — that conversation doesn't wait. The Decompression Window is a tool, not a rule that overrides your judgment.
