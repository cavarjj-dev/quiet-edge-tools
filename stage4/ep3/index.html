The Process Contract

A 3-week attention training framework for high-stakes competition

Stage 4 Ep. 3 Athlete Edition

The core problem: When results carry weight — scouts, showcases, starting spots — your brain tries to run two processes at once: execution and evaluation. It can't. That split is where trained skills break down.

The fix: The Process Contract pre-decides where your attention goes before the pressure arrives. You're not making that choice mid-competition — you already made it. Three parts. Three weeks. One session at a time.

Step 1 — Pre-Session Contract
Name one process behavior before each session. This becomes your anchor.

Choose one thing you control — a specific behavior, not a result or a stat. Not "play well." Not "hit my shots." Something like "defensive footwork on every drive" or "my approach angle on grounders." That's your contract for the session.

Tap a chip above to auto-fill, or write your own. One behavior only — specificity is what makes it work.

Why this works: You've already decided where your attention goes. When the scoreboard starts pulling — and it will — you have somewhere specific to return to. That decision was made in calm, not in chaos.

3
Step 2 — Mid-Competition Anchor
A short phrase to return to your contract when results start pulling

When you catch yourself monitoring results mid-game, you need a phrase that redirects — not motivational, not calming. Just a cue that returns your attention to the contract behavior.

Next play. Track the assignment.
One possession. My job.
Back to my thing.
Process. Here. Now.
Trust the rep.
My behavior. My control.

Short, personal, and action-oriented. You'll say this to yourself — not to anyone else.

Week 1 focus: Just notice. You're building the habit of pre-deciding — don't expect to feel a shift yet. Log every session, practice or game.

Week 2 focus: Track when you returned to your anchor — and when you didn't. Naming the moments of drift builds the awareness that eventually prevents them.

Week 3 focus: The compound effect. Most athletes notice cleaner decision-making here — not from trying harder, but from the attention habit becoming automatic.

Grading guide: 5 = Held the contract all session. 4 = Returned to it within 1–2 plays of drift. 3 = Noticed drift mid-session, corrected some. 2 = Mostly outcome-focused, some return. 1 = Lost the contract early — didn't return. You don't need a 5 to compound. You need to keep logging.

Week 2 Calibration
Complete after your 7th session — before starting Week 2

1. What moments triggered outcome-monitoring most?

Name the specific situations — down by 2, coach watching, missed assignment. The more precise, the more useful.

2. How specific is your process behavior?

If it's broad ("play hard"), narrow it. What exact moment, exact movement, exact decision does it target?

3. Did your anchor phrase actually work?

If yes — when? If no — what would be more natural to say to yourself in that moment?

Week 3 Calibration
Complete after your final session of the 3-week cycle

1. Did the quality of your execution change?

Not the result — the quality of your decisions and movement under pressure. Describe what you noticed.

2. What specific moment felt most different than 3 weeks ago?

One game or practice moment where you handled pressure differently. Be specific.

3. What's your process behavior for the next 3-week cycle?

Build on what you learned. Keep the same behavior if it needs more depth, or layer in a second one.
