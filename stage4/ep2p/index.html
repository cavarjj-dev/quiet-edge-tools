Step 3 of 3 — After 3–4 Weeks

Read the Pattern

The Language Shift

Most parent language before competition prescribes an energy state. Here is the exchange from this episode — the exact words you can use instead.

Situation — Your athlete is quiet and flat in the car before a showcase game.
Most parents say
"Come on, you need to get your energy up. This is a big game."
Say instead
"You seem settled. That's worked for some of your best performances. Stay there."
Why: you stop prescribing an energy state and start affirming what they're already doing — which may be exactly right.

Why This Works

Hanin (2000) — Each athlete has a unique Zone of Optimal Functioning. There is no universal "right" energy level. Prescribing one — even with good intent — pulls the athlete away from their personal zone.

Feldman (2007) — A parent's emotional state is transmitted to the athlete through tone, pacing, and body language before a single word is spoken. A calm, non-prescriptive parent presence reduces competition anxiety without intervention.

Mageau & Vallerand (2003) — Athletes with autonomy-supportive parents show lower pre-competition anxiety and higher intrinsic motivation. Autonomy support means acknowledging feelings without redirecting them.

Research sources: Hanin, Y.L. (2000). Emotions in sport. Human Kinetics. · Feldman, R. (2007). Developmental Psychology, 43(2). · Mageau & Vallerand (2003). Journal of Sports Sciences, 21(11).
