Choose Your Scenario

Elite athletes don't visualize vaguely. They target specific scenarios. Which situation do you most need to pre-rehearse?

Why this matters: Visualization is only as powerful as it is specific. A vague "I see myself winning" produces weak neural encoding. A detailed, scenario-specific script produces functional equivalence — your brain treats it as a real rep.

Step 3 of 4

Adversity + Recovery

This is where most athletes stop — and why most visualization doesn't transfer. Pre-rehearsing mistakes and recovery builds a separate, critical neural track.

The research on this: Standard mental imagery only rehearses success. Adversity visualization pre-encodes the recovery response — so when the mistake actually happens, your brain has already been there and knows what to do. This is the difference between visualization and wishful thinking.
The Mistake

What could realistically go wrong?

Pick one specific, realistic mistake that could happen in your scenario. Not catastrophizing — a real possibility.

The 3-Second Recovery Response

Exactly what happens next — in your body, your mind, your next action?

Be specific about each of the three channels. Write it in present tense.
