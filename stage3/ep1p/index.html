Practice Quality Checker

Are you recognizing the right things after practice?
The research on deliberate practice is clear: volume alone doesn't build expertise. Four specific elements separate skill-building sessions from activity accumulation — and as a parent, the questions you ask after practice signal which ones you value. This tool helps you see what you're currently noticing, and gives you better questions to start using today.
Step 1

Step 2

Which elements were present?

For each of the 4 markers of deliberate practice, indicate what you observed or heard about from your child. Be honest — "Unsure" is a useful data point too.

●
Full Attention Focused and engaged — not distracted, not just going through the motions. Mental presence, not just physical presence.
●
Working on Weaknesses Practicing what's hard — the parts they struggle with — not just repeating what they're already good at. There was visible struggle or frustration.
●
Getting Feedback Received specific correction or input — from a coach, video review, or their own self-awareness. Not just general praise or generic encouragement.
●
Making Adjustments After getting feedback, they tried something differently. Not just repeating the same thing — they made an actual change in approach or execution.
Step 3

What did you say after practice?

Write the first thing you actually said to your child after this practice ended (in the car, walking out, at dinner — whatever came first). Don't edit it for how you wish you'd responded.
